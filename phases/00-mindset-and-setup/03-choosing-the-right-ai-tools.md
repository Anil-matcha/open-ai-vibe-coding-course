# 0.3 Choosing the Right AI Tools

> "A carpenter doesn't use every tool in the hardware store. They use the right tool for the job."

There are hundreds of AI tools launching every week. This lesson cuts through the noise and helps you pick the **2-3 tools you'll actually use every day** — based on your budget, your goals, and what you're building.

By the end of this lesson, you'll have your core AI stack set up and ready to go.

---

## The LaunchOS Tool Selection Philosophy

Before we look at specific tools, internalize these three rules:

### Rule 1: Depth Over Breadth
Using 1 tool well beats using 10 tools poorly. Pick your core AI assistant and learn it inside out before adding another.

### Rule 2: Start Free, Upgrade When It Hurts
Every major AI tool has a free tier. Use it until you hit a wall. When you find yourself constantly hitting limits that slow you down, that's when you upgrade.

### Rule 3: The Tool Doesn't Matter — The Workflow Does
ChatGPT vs. Claude vs. Gemini debates are mostly irrelevant for beginners. What matters is that you **use AI consistently in a structured way**. Pick one and commit.

## The Three Categories of AI Tools You Need

### Category 1: Your AI Co-Founder (The General-Purpose Assistant)

This is the tool you'll have open all day. It brainstorms, writes, analyzes, and helps you think through problems.

| Tool | Best For | Free Tier | Paid Tier | LaunchOS Verdict |
|------|----------|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| [ChatGPT](https://chatgpt.com) | All-around business tasks, Custom GPTs, image generation | GPT-4o mini, limited | $20/mo — GPT-4o, DALL-E, file uploads | **Best all-rounder.** Start here if you can only pick one |
| [Claude](https://claude.ai) | Long-form writing, analysis, nuanced reasoning | Claude Sonnet, limited | $20/mo — Claude Opus, longer context | **Best for writing & complex thinking.** Outputs feel more natural |
| [Gemini](https://gemini.google.com) | Google ecosystem, research, multimodal | Gemini Pro, generous | $20/mo — Ultra model, workspace integration | **Best if you live in Google Workspace** |

#### 🎯 How to Choose

Ask yourself one question: **"What will I use AI for MOST?"**

- **"I need help with everything — ideas, writing, research, planning"** → Start with **ChatGPT**
- **"I'll mostly write content — blogs, emails, landing pages"** → Start with **Claude**
- **"I already use Google Docs and Gmail for everything"** → Start with **Gemini**

> **Pro tip:** You don't have to marry one tool. Most experienced users have ChatGPT as their primary and Claude for long-form writing. But start with ONE.

### Category 2: Your AI Researcher (Search & Analysis)

Your general AI assistant has a knowledge cutoff and can't browse the web (or does it poorly). You need a research tool for live market data, competitor analysis, and finding current statistics.

| Tool | Best For | Free Tier | Paid Tier | LaunchOS Verdict |
|------|----------|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| [Perplexity](https://perplexity.ai) | Deep research with citations | 5 Pro searches/day | $20/mo — unlimited Pro searches | **The #1 research tool.** Replaces Google for business research |
| [ChatGPT with browsing](https://chatgpt.com) | Quick web lookups | Included in free tier | Included in Plus | Good enough for casual research |
| Google (yes, regular Google) | Finding specific websites, checking facts | Free | Free | Still essential for verification |

#### 🎯 How to Choose

- If your business involves **heavy market research** (SaaS, services) → Get **Perplexity**
- If you just need **occasional fact-checking** → Use **ChatGPT's web browsing** feature
- **Always verify important claims** by visiting the source yourself

### Category 3: Your AI Creative Suite (Design & Visual)

You need visuals — logos, social media graphics, product mockups, website elements. You don't need a design degree.

| Tool | Best For | Free Tier | Paid Tier | LaunchOS Verdict |
|------|----------|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| [Canva](https://canva.com) | Social posts, logos, presentations, everything visual | Very generous | $15/mo — AI features, premium templates | **Start here.** The Swiss Army knife of design |
| [Midjourney](https://midjourney.com) | Stunning AI-generated images | None | $10/mo | Best image quality, but requires Discord |
| [DALL-E (via ChatGPT)](https://chatgpt.com) | Quick image generation inside your chat | Included with ChatGPT | Included with Plus | Most convenient if you already use ChatGPT |

#### 🎯 How to Choose

- **Everyone** should set up **Canva** (free tier is excellent)
- Add **Midjourney** if your brand relies heavily on unique imagery
- Use **DALL-E** for quick, throwaway images during brainstorming

## 🧪 Exercise: Set Up Your Stack

### Step 1: Choose Your Budget

Circle your budget:
- [ ] $0/month — I'm bootstrapping hard
- [ ] ~$20/month — I can invest the price of a few coffees
- [ ] ~$60/month — I'm serious and ready to invest

### Step 2: Create Your Accounts

Based on the stack that matches your budget above, create accounts for each tool. Don't just bookmark them — **actually sign up and log in.**

### Step 3: Run a Test Drive

Open your AI Co-Founder tool and paste this prompt:

```text
I just signed up for you as my AI business assistant. I'm going to be using you
extensively over the next 8-12 weeks to build an online business.

To calibrate our working relationship, please:

1. Explain your top 3 strengths in a business context
2. Warn me about your top 3 limitations I should watch out for
3. Suggest 3 ways I can get the best results from you
4. Create a short "working agreement" between us — a set of rules for how
   we'll work together effectively

Write this in a conversational, honest tone. Don't oversell yourself.
```

### Step 4: Save the Output

Copy the AI's response into your LaunchOS Journal under a section called **"My AI Co-Founder Agreement."**

This is more useful than it sounds — it creates a mental framework for how you'll interact with AI throughout the course.

### Step 5: Test Your Research Tool

Open Perplexity (or your chosen research tool) and search:

```text
What are the most profitable types of online businesses to start in 2025
with less than $100 in initial investment? Give me specific examples with
estimated revenue ranges.
```

Compare the results to what you'd get from regular Google. Notice how much more useful cited, synthesized answers are.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | What to Do Instead |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Subscribing to 5+ AI tools at once | You'll waste money and feel overwhelmed | Start with 1-2, add more only when you hit a specific limitation |
| Using free tier forever when it's limiting you | You lose hours working around restrictions when $20/mo would save you 10+ hours | Track how often you hit limits; if it's daily, upgrade |
| Ignoring the research category | Your AI co-founder makes things up; you need a fact-checker | Always have a research tool for verification |
| Picking a tool because an influencer said so | Their use case isn't your use case | Pick based on YOUR primary task (writing, research, design) |
| Not configuring custom instructions | You'll give the same context over and over | Set up custom instructions/system prompts on Day 1 (covered next lesson) |

## Setting Up Custom Instructions (Quick Win)

Most AI tools let you set "custom instructions" that apply to every conversation. Do this NOW to save yourself from repeating context.

### ChatGPT Custom Instructions

Go to Settings → Personalization → Custom Instructions and paste:

```text
About me:
I'm building an online business using the LaunchOS framework. I'm a [beginner/
intermediate] entrepreneur. My business model is [pick from: digital products,
productized services, media/community, or software/SaaS — or "undecided"].
I have [X] hours per week and a budget of [$X/month] for tools.

How I want you to respond:
- Be practical and actionable, not theoretical
- Give me specific steps, not vague advice
- Challenge my assumptions when I'm wrong
- Use real-world examples from successful businesses
- Format outputs with headers, bullets, and tables for easy scanning
- If I ask for something you're not confident about, tell me instead of guessing
```

### Claude Project Instructions

Create a new Project in Claude and set the project instructions to similar content as above.
