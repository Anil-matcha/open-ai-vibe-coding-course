# 1.6 Idea Validation Framework

> "The most expensive mistake in business is building something nobody wants. The cheapest way to avoid it is to ask."

You have a problem, market research, and competitor analysis. Before you build ANYTHING, you must validate that real people will pay real money for your solution.

This lesson teaches you how to test your idea with minimal time and zero code. You'll learn to separate polite encouragement ("That's a cool idea!") from genuine buying intent ("How do I sign up?").

---

## The Validation Spectrum

Not all validation is created equal. Here's how different signals rank:

| Validation Method | Strength | What It Proves |
|------------------|----------|---------------|
| 😐 "I asked my friends and they liked it" | ❌ Worthless | People are polite |
| 😐 Social media poll / survey | ⚠️ Weak | People have opinions (opinions ≠ money) |
| 🙂 Strangers say "I'd use that" in an interview | ⚠️ Moderate | Interest exists, but talk is cheap |
| 😊 People sign up for a waitlist | ✅ Good | They took an action (email = minor commitment) |
| 😃 People share it / refer others | ✅ Strong | They believe in it enough to risk their reputation |
| 🤩 People pre-order or put down a deposit | 🔥 Excellent | Money talks. This is real validation |
| 🏆 People pay for a prototype/beta | 💰 Gold standard | You have a business |

**Your goal in this lesson: Get to at least "waitlist" level validation.**

## Validation Method 1: Customer Discovery Interviews

Talk to 10 people who match your target customer profile. This is the fastest way to learn if your problem is real and your solution makes sense.

### The Interview Rules

| Rule | Why |
|------|-----|
| **Talk to strangers, not friends** | Friends lie to protect your feelings |
| **Ask about their problem, NOT your solution** | You're testing the problem, not pitching |
| **Shut up and listen** | You should talk 20% of the time, they talk 80% |
| **Never ask "Would you buy this?"** | It's a leading question — everyone says yes |
| **Ask about past behavior, not future intentions** | "Have you paid for X?" > "Would you pay for X?" |

### The 10-Question Interview Script

```
WARM-UP (2 minutes):
1. "Tell me about your role and what a typical week looks like."

PROBLEM EXPLORATION (5 minutes):
2. "What's the most time-consuming or frustrating part of [relevant activity]?"
3. "How often does this problem come up?"
4. "Walk me through the last time this happened. What did you do?"
5. "What have you tried to solve this? (Tools, workarounds, hiring someone)"

PAIN DEPTH (3 minutes):
6. "How much time/money does this problem cost you per week/month?"
7. "On a scale of 1-10, how painful is this in your daily work?"
8. "If you could wave a magic wand and fix ONE thing, what would it be?"

SOLUTION TESTING (3 minutes):
9. "If something existed that [brief description of your solution], 
    how would that change things for you?"
10. "What would you need to see to feel confident trying something new?"

CLOSE (2 minutes):
"This has been incredibly helpful. I'm working on something in this space. 
Can I reach out when I have something to show you?"
```

### How to Find Interview Subjects

| Method | Where | Expected Response Rate |
|--------|-------|----------------------|
| LinkedIn cold message | Send 50 messages to your ICP | 5-10 will agree |
| Reddit post | "Researching [topic] — anyone willing to chat?" | 3-5 responses |
| Twitter/X reply | Reply to people discussing the problem | 2-4 responses |
| Facebook Groups | Post in relevant groups (ask permission first) | 5-10 responses |
| Warm introductions | Ask friends/colleagues for intros | 3-5 intros |

### AI Prompt: Interview Preparation

```text
I'm conducting customer discovery interviews for a business that solves
this problem:
[paste your problem statement]

My target customer is:
[paste your ICP]

Help me prepare:

1. Write a LinkedIn outreach message (under 100 words) that I can send
   to potential interview subjects. Make it warm, not salesy. I'm NOT
   selling anything — I'm genuinely researching.

2. Customize my 10-question interview script for this specific problem.
   Add 3 follow-up questions I should have ready if the conversation
   goes well.

3. Create an interview notes template I can fill out during or right
   after each conversation.

4. List 5 "green flags" (signals that validate my idea) and 5 "red flags"
   (signals that invalidate it) to watch for during interviews.
```

### After the Interviews: Analysis

```text
I conducted [X] customer discovery interviews. Here are my summarized notes:

Interview 1: [key findings]
Interview 2: [key findings]
Interview 3: [key findings]
...

Analyze the patterns:
1. What problems came up most frequently?
2. How severe is the pain (on average)?
3. What do they currently spend on solutions?
4. Did anyone volunteer to be a beta tester or early customer?
5. Based on these interviews, should I proceed with my idea? Why or why not?
6. How should I refine my idea based on what I learned?
```

## Validation Method 2: The "Smoke Test" Landing Page

A smoke test landing page looks like a real product page but doesn't have a product behind it. You measure how many people sign up — that's your demand signal.

### What You Need

| Element | Purpose | Tool |
|---------|---------|------|
| **Headline** | Grab attention, state the value proposition | AI-generated, you edit |
| **Subheadline** | Explain what it does in one sentence | AI-generated, you edit |
| **3 Benefits** | Why should they care? | From your research |
| **Call to Action** | "Join the waitlist" or "Get early access" | Button → email capture |
| **Email capture form** | Collect emails as proof of demand | Google Forms, Typeform, or built-in |

### AI Prompt: Landing Page Copy

```text
I'm creating a landing page to validate demand for my business idea.

THE PROBLEM: [paste your problem statement]
THE SOLUTION: [one-sentence description of your solution]
THE TARGET CUSTOMER: [paste your ICP]
THE PRICE POINT: [estimated price or "free beta"]

Write landing page copy with these sections:
1. HEADLINE: A bold, benefit-driven headline (under 10 words)
   Give me 5 options to choose from.
2. SUBHEADLINE: One sentence that explains the product (under 25 words)
3. THREE KEY BENEFITS: Each with a heading and 1-2 sentence description
4. SOCIAL PROOF PLACEHOLDER: A line about what I can use before I have
   real testimonials
5. CALL TO ACTION: Button text and supporting text
   (Goal: get email signups for waitlist)
6. FAQ: 4 questions a skeptical visitor would have, with brief answers

Tone: Confident but not hype-y. Like a smart friend recommending something.
```

### Building the Landing Page

**Option A: Carrd (Recommended — 30 minutes)**
1. Go to [carrd.co](https://carrd.co)
2. Pick a simple template
3. Replace the text with your AI-generated copy
4. Add a form element connected to your email (free Google Form works)
5. Publish

**Option B: Google Forms + Link (Free — 15 minutes)**
1. Create a Google Form with: Name, Email, "What's your biggest challenge with [problem]?"
2. Write a brief pitch above the form
3. Share the link directly

### How to Drive Traffic to Your Landing Page

| Channel | Method | Expected Signups | Effort |
|---------|--------|-----------------|--------|
| **Your existing network** | Share on personal social media, email friends | 5-20 | Low |
| **Reddit** | Post in relevant subreddits (follow rules, don't spam) | 10-50 | Medium |
| **Facebook Groups** | Share in relevant groups (add genuine value, not just links) | 5-30 | Medium |
| **Twitter/X** | Thread about the problem, link in bio | 5-30 | Medium |
| **LinkedIn** | Post about the problem you're solving | 10-50 | Medium |
| **Cold outreach** | Email/DM 50 people in your ICP | 5-15 | High |

### How to Read the Results

After 1-2 weeks of sharing:

| Metric | Poor | Decent | Great |
|--------|------|--------|-------|
| Landing page visitors | < 50 | 50-200 | 200+ |
| Email signup rate | < 3% | 3-10% | 10%+ |
| Total signups | < 10 | 10-50 | 50+ |

**Interpretation:**
- **< 3% signup rate:** The problem or positioning isn't resonating. Revisit your copy or your ICP.
- **3-10% signup rate:** Decent interest. Proceed cautiously — refine the idea.
- **10%+ signup rate:** Strong signal. Move forward with confidence.
- **People sharing it without being asked:** You've struck a nerve. Build this.

## Validation Method 3: The Pre-Sell Test

The strongest validation: someone gives you money before the product exists.

### How to Pre-Sell

| Approach | Script | When to Use |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| **"Founding members" discount** | "I'm building [product]. The first 10 customers get 50% off forever." | Digital products, SaaS |
| **"Done-for-you" pilot** | "I'll do this for you manually first, then build the tool." | Services, productized services |
| **Pre-order** | "Pre-order for $X. Ships in [timeframe]. Full refund if you're not happy." | Digital products, templates |
| **Workshop/beta group** | "Join my beta cohort for $X. Help shape the product and get lifetime access." | Courses, communities |

### AI Prompt: Pre-Sell Strategy

```text
I want to pre-sell my product before it's built to validate demand.

MY PRODUCT: [description]
MY TARGET CUSTOMER: [ICP]
MY ESTIMATED PRICE: $[price]
MY BUSINESS MODEL: [one-time / subscription / service]

Help me:
1. Choose the best pre-sell approach for my specific product type
2. Write a pre-sell pitch I can send to people on my waitlist
3. Determine a fair "founding member" discount that validates demand
   without devaluing the product
4. Create a simple guarantee/refund policy that reduces risk for buyers
5. Write 3 follow-up emails for people who showed interest but didn't buy
6. Set a realistic goal: How many pre-sales would validate this idea?
```

## The Validation Decision Matrix

After running your validation tests, use this matrix to decide what to do next:

| Interview Results | Landing Page Results | Pre-Sell Results | Decision |
|------------------|---------------------|-----------------|----------|
| 7+ out of 10 said the problem is severe | 10%+ signup rate | Any pre-sales at all | 🚀 **GO! Build this.** |
| 5-7 said it's a real problem | 5-10% signup rate | No pre-sales yet | ⚠️ **Refine.** Adjust positioning or ICP |
| 3-5 said it's "kind of" a problem | 3-5% signup rate | N/A | 🔄 **Pivot.** The problem may be real but your angle is wrong |
| < 3 related to the problem | < 3% signup rate | N/A | ❌ **Drop it.** This problem isn't painful enough to build on |

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | What to Do Instead |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Only asking friends and family | They'll tell you what you want to hear | Interview strangers who match your ICP |
| Asking "Would you buy this?" | Leading question — everyone says yes | Ask about past behavior and current spending |
| Building before validating | You waste weeks or months | Build NOTHING until you have 10+ interview signals |
| Getting discouraged by a low signup rate | Maybe your copy is bad, not your idea | Test 3 different headlines before concluding the idea is bad |
| Ignoring negative validation signals | Sunk cost fallacy — you've invested time so you don't want to quit | Negative signals save you months. Pivot or drop faster |
| Over-validating and never starting | "Just 10 more interviews..." becomes procrastination | Set a deadline: 2 weeks maximum for validation |
