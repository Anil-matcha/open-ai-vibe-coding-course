# 1.5 Competitor Research Framework

> "If you know the enemy and know yourself, you need not fear the result of a hundred battles." — Sun Tzu

Competition is good news. It means the market is real and people are paying. Your job isn't to avoid competition — it's to find the **gap** that competitors are missing and own it.

This lesson gives you a systematic framework for analyzing competitors, mapping the landscape, and finding your unfair advantage.

---

## Why Competitor Research Matters

| Without Competitor Research | With Competitor Research |
|----------------------------|------------------------|
| "I think my idea is unique!" | "I know exactly who I'm competing with and why I'm different" |
| Build features nobody asked for | Build the features competitors' customers are begging for |
| Price randomly | Price strategically based on market positioning |
| Discover competitors after launch (panic) | Plan around them before launch (strategy) |

---

## The Competitor Research Stack (3 Layers)

### Layer 1: Direct Competitors

Products or services that solve the **same problem** for the **same audience** in a **similar way**.

> Example: If you're building a project management tool for freelancers, your direct competitors are Trello, Asana, Monday.com (for freelancers).

### Layer 2: Indirect Competitors

Products that solve the **same problem** but in a **different way**.

> Example: For freelance project management, indirect competitors include spreadsheets, email chains, paper planners, or even a virtual assistant handling it manually.

### Layer 3: Substitute Solutions

Things people do **instead of** solving the problem. These are often "do nothing" or "manual workaround" solutions.

> Example: Freelancers who just "keep it all in their head" or use sticky notes.

**You must understand all three layers.** Most founders only look at Layer 1 and miss the biggest threats.

## Step 1: Find Your Competitors

### AI Prompt: Competitor Discovery

```text
I'm building a solution for this problem:
[paste your problem statement]

My target customer is:
[paste your ICP from Lesson 1.4]

Find my competitors across 3 layers:

LAYER 1 - DIRECT COMPETITORS (same problem, same audience, similar solution):
List 5-10 products/services. Include their name, URL, pricing, and one-sentence
description.

LAYER 2 - INDIRECT COMPETITORS (same problem, different approach):
List 3-5 alternative approaches people use. Explain how each works.

LAYER 3 - SUBSTITUTES (what people do instead of solving the problem):
List 2-3 "non-solutions" (including doing nothing). Explain why people
stick with these.

For each Layer 1 competitor, rate them on a scale of 1-10 for:
- Market presence (how well-known are they?)
- Product quality (how good is their solution?)
- Threat level (how hard will they be to compete with?)
```

### Manual Verification

After AI gives you the list, verify and expand it:

1. **Google search:** "[your problem] tool/software/service" — check the first 3 pages
2. **G2/Capterra search:** Search for your product category
3. **Product Hunt:** Search for related products launched in the last 2 years
4. **AlternativeTo.net:** Enter a known competitor and find alternatives
5. **App stores:** If relevant, search iOS/Android app stores

### AI Prompt: Competitor Deep Dive

```text
I need a detailed analysis of [competitor name] (URL: [competitor URL]).
They are a direct competitor to the business I'm planning to build.

Analyze them on these dimensions:

1. PRODUCT: What is their core product? What features do they offer?
   What's their key differentiator? What do they do well? What do they do poorly?

2. PRICING: What are their pricing tiers? What's included at each level?
   Is there a free tier? What pricing model do they use?

3. CUSTOMERS: Who is their target audience? What customer segments do they serve?
   Based on reviews (G2, Trustpilot, app stores), what do customers love?
   What do customers complain about?

4. MARKETING: How do they acquire customers? What content do they publish?
   What's their positioning and key messaging?

5. SWOT: Provide a SWOT analysis from MY perspective as a new entrant.

6. GAP ANALYSIS: Identify 3 specific gaps in their offering that I could exploit.
   These should be things their customers want but don't get.

Be specific. Don't give me generic marketing speak. Give me actionable intelligence.
```

## Step 3: Map the Competitive Landscape

Now visualize where all competitors sit relative to each other. This reveals the whitespace — the position nobody has claimed.

### The 2x2 Positioning Matrix

Choose 2 dimensions that matter most to your target customer and plot competitors on a grid.

**Common dimension pairs:**

| Dimension 1 | Dimension 2 | Good For |
|-------------|-------------|----------|
| Simple ↔ Complex | Cheap ↔ Expensive | Finding the "simple + affordable" gap |
| General ↔ Specialized | Self-serve ↔ Done-for-you | Finding niche opportunities |
| Beginners ↔ Experts | Basic features ↔ Advanced features | Finding underserved skill levels |

### AI Prompt: Positioning Matrix

```text
I've analyzed these competitors in the [your niche] space:

Competitor 1: [name] - [one-sentence description]
Competitor 2: [name] - [one-sentence description]
Competitor 3: [name] - [one-sentence description]
Competitor 4: [name] - [one-sentence description]
Competitor 5: [name] - [one-sentence description]

Create a 2x2 positioning matrix for these competitors using these dimensions:
- X-axis: [your chosen dimension, e.g., "Simple to use ↔ Complex/Feature-rich"]
- Y-axis: [your chosen dimension, e.g., "Affordable ↔ Premium"]

For each competitor, place them on the grid and explain why.

Then identify: Where is the EMPTY QUADRANT — the position that no one occupies?
Is that empty quadrant a viable position for a new entrant? Why or why not?
```

### How to Find Your Position

The best position for a new entrant is usually one of these:

| Strategy | Description | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **The Specialist** | Same quality, but focused on one niche | "Asana, but specifically for agencies" |
| **The Simplifier** | Fewer features, dramatically easier to use | "Notion, but for people who find Notion overwhelming" |
| **The Budget Play** | Similar quality, much cheaper | "Calendly, but free for solopreneurs" |
| **The Premium Play** | Much better quality, higher price | "Fiverr, but with vetted professionals and guaranteed quality" |
| **The Differentiator** | Solve the same problem in a fundamentally different way | "Project management through async video updates, not kanban boards" |

## Step 4: Identify Your Competitive Advantage

Now answer the hardest question: **Why would someone choose YOU over the established players?**

### AI Prompt: Finding Your Edge

```text
Based on my competitor analysis:

My competitors are: [list top 3 with brief descriptions]
Their weaknesses are: [list key weaknesses you found]
My target customer is: [your ICP]
My skills and advantages are: [your background, skills, unique access]

Help me identify my potential competitive advantage by exploring these angles:

1. NICHE SPECIALIZATION: Is there a specific sub-group of the market that
   competitors serve poorly? Who are they and why are they underserved?

2. EXPERIENCE GAP: Based on customer complaints, what experience
   improvements could I focus on?

3. PRICING GAP: Is there a pricing model or price point that no one
   is offering? (Too expensive for small users? Too cheap for quality?)

4. TECHNOLOGY GAP: Are competitors using outdated approaches? Could AI
   or automation give me an unfair advantage?

5. DISTRIBUTION GAP: Are there channels to reach customers that
   competitors aren't using?

Rank these 5 angles from strongest to weakest for MY specific situation.
Explain why.
```

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | What to Do Instead |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| "I have no competitors!" | Either you haven't looked, or there's no market | Dig deeper — everyone has competitors |
| Only analyzing direct competitors | You miss the real threat (indirect competitors and substitutes) | Analyze all 3 layers |
| Getting intimidated by big competitors | Big companies have big blind spots | Focus on what they do POORLY, not what they do well |
| Copying competitors instead of differentiating | "Me too" products don't win | Find the gap, don't fill the same space |
| Doing competitor research once and never again | The landscape changes constantly | Revisit quarterly, or when a new player emerges |
| Spending weeks on competitor research | Diminishing returns after the first 3-4 hours | Set a time limit: 3 hours maximum for this exercise |
