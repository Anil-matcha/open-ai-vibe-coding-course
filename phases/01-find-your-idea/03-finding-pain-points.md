# 1.3 Finding Pain Points

> "Your next business idea is hiding in someone's frustrated Reddit post, negative app review, or exasperated Google search."

In the last lesson, you learned how to think about problems. In this lesson, you'll learn **where to find them**. We cover 6 proven methods — from mining online conversations to using AI as your research assistant.

By the end, you'll have a list of 10+ real, validated pain points to work with.

---

## Method 1: The Community Mining Method

People openly complain about their problems in online communities. All you have to do is listen.

### Where to Mine

| Platform | Best For | How to Search |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| **Reddit** | Every niche imaginable | Search subreddits for complaint phrases |
| **Facebook Groups** | Local businesses, hobbyists, parents | Join relevant groups and read posts |
| **LinkedIn** | B2B problems, professional frustrations | Search posts and comments |
| **Twitter/X** | Real-time frustrations, industry drama | Search keywords + filter for complaints |
| **Discord/Slack** | Niche professional communities | Join and lurk |
| **Quora** | "How do I...?" problems | Search by topic |

### The Magic Search Phrases

These phrases signal real pain. Search for them in your target community:

```
"I'm so frustrated with..."
"Is there a tool that..."
"Does anyone know how to..."
"I can't believe there isn't..."
"I've been looking for..."
"Why is it so hard to..."
"I hate when..."
"Spent hours trying to..."
"There has to be a better way..."
"I'd pay money for..."
```

### Step-by-Step: Reddit Mining

1. Go to [reddit.com](https://reddit.com)
2. Find 3-5 subreddits related to your target audience (e.g., r/freelance, r/smallbusiness, r/realestate)
3. Search each subreddit for the magic phrases above
4. Read the top 20 posts for each search
5. Copy the most painful complaints into a spreadsheet:

| Subreddit | Post Title | Problem Description | Upvotes | Comments | Pain Level |
|-----------|-----------|---------------------|---------|----------|------------|
| r/freelance | "Spent 3 hours on a proposal for a $500 job" | Proposals take too long and most don't convert | 342 | 89 | 🔥 High |

### AI Prompt: Community Mining Assistant

```text
I'm researching pain points in the [your niche] space. I've found these
complaints from online communities:

1. [paste complaint 1]
2. [paste complaint 2]
3. [paste complaint 3]
4. [paste complaint 4]
5. [paste complaint 5]

For each complaint:
1. Identify the core underlying problem (not the surface-level frustration)
2. Estimate how many people likely have this problem
3. Rate the pain level (1-10)
4. Suggest what type of solution could fix it (tool, template, service, course)
5. Note if there are already solutions for this (and why they're insufficient)
```

## Method 2: The Review Mining Method

Product reviews — especially 2-star and 3-star reviews — are a goldmine. One-star reviews are often rage-venting. Five-star reviews are useless. But 2-3 star reviews usually contain specific, thoughtful criticism of what's wrong.

### Where to Find Reviews

| Platform | What to Review |
|----------|---------------|
| [G2.com](https://g2.com) | Software and SaaS tools |
| [Capterra](https://capterra.com) | Business software |
| [Trustpilot](https://trustpilot.com) | All types of businesses |
| [Amazon](https://amazon.com) | Physical products, books, courses |
| [App Store / Google Play](https://play.google.com) | Mobile apps |
| [Product Hunt](https://producthunt.com) | New tech products (read the comments) |

### Step-by-Step: Review Mining

1. Go to G2.com or Capterra
2. Search for popular tools in your target space
3. Filter reviews to 2-star and 3-star only
4. Read 30+ reviews and look for recurring themes
5. Document each theme in your spreadsheet:

| Tool Reviewed | Recurring Complaint | How Many Mentions | Business Opportunity |
|--------------|--------------------|--------------------|---------------------|
| Calendly | "Too expensive for small teams" | 15+ reviews | Cheaper scheduling for solopreneurs |
| Trello | "Gets messy with large projects" | 20+ reviews | Simpler project management for freelancers |

### AI Prompt: Review Analysis

```text
I've collected these reviews from [tool name] on G2.com. All are 2-3 star reviews:

[Paste 10 reviews]

Analyze these reviews and:
1. Identify the 5 most common complaint themes
2. For each theme, count how many reviews mention it
3. Determine which complaints represent genuine product gaps vs. user error
4. Suggest a business opportunity based on the top 3 product gaps
5. Write a one-sentence "problem statement" for each opportunity
```

## Method 3: The "Look in the Mirror" Method

Your own daily frustrations are the fastest path to a business idea — because you already deeply understand the problem.

### The Self-Audit Worksheet

Complete this worksheet in your journal:

```
MY DAILY FRUSTRATIONS AUDIT
═══════════════════════════

My current/recent job: _______________
My hobbies/side activities: _______________

WORK FRUSTRATIONS:
1. What task do I dread doing every week?
   _______________________________________________
2. What Excel/spreadsheet do I update manually?
   _______________________________________________
3. What process do I say "there has to be a better way" about?
   _______________________________________________
4. What do my coworkers complain about most?
   _______________________________________________
5. What tool at work do we all hate but keep using?
   _______________________________________________

PERSONAL FRUSTRATIONS:
6. What recurring life task takes way too long?
   _______________________________________________
7. What have I Googled a solution for and not found one?
   _______________________________________________
8. What service have I wished existed but couldn't find?
   _______________________________________________

MONEY FRUSTRATIONS:
9. What am I paying too much for?
   _______________________________________________
10. Where have I wasted money on bad solutions?
    _______________________________________________
```

### AI Prompt: Personal Problem Expansion

```text
I completed a personal frustrations audit. Here are my top 3 frustrations:

1. [paste frustration 1]
2. [paste frustration 2]
3. [paste frustration 3]

For each frustration:
1. Do other people commonly share this frustration? (Give evidence)
2. What existing solutions exist? (Name specific products/services)
3. Why might existing solutions be inadequate?
4. What would a 10x better solution look like?
5. Could this be turned into a viable business? Why or why not?
```

## Method 4: The Trend Surfing Method

New trends create new problems. When a technology, platform, or behavior explodes in popularity, the people using it inevitably need help.

### Current Trend → Problem Examples

| Trend | New Problem It Creates | Business Opportunity |
|-------|----------------------|---------------------|
| AI tools going mainstream | People don't know how to use AI effectively | AI training, templates, workflows |
| Remote work permanence | Managing distributed teams is hard | Remote team culture tools, async communication |
| Short-form video dominance | Businesses need constant video content | Video editing services, template packs |
| Creator economy growth | Creators need to manage business operations | Creator finance tools, contract templates |
| No-code tools | People build apps but can't design or market them | No-code design services, launch playbooks |

### Where to Spot Trends

| Source | URL | What to Look For |
|--------|-----|-----------------|
| Google Trends | [trends.google.com](https://trends.google.com) | Rising search terms in your space |
| Exploding Topics | [explodingtopics.com](https://explodingtopics.com) | Topics growing rapidly before they peak |
| Product Hunt | [producthunt.com](https://producthunt.com) | What new products are launching and in what categories |
| Hacker News | [news.ycombinator.com](https://news.ycombinator.com) | What the tech community is buzzing about |
| Twitter/X Trends | [twitter.com/explore](https://twitter.com/explore) | Real-time conversations and pain points |

### AI Prompt: Trend-to-Problem Analysis

```text
I've identified this emerging trend: [describe the trend, e.g., "Solo founders
using AI to build SaaS products without coding"].

Analyze this trend and:
1. List 10 NEW problems this trend creates for the people involved
2. For each problem, rate how urgently it needs solving (1-10)
3. Note which problems currently have NO good solution
4. Suggest what type of business could solve each unsolved problem
5. Which of these opportunities has the best risk/reward ratio for a solo founder?
```

## Method 5: The Expert Interview Method

Talk to people who work in your target industry. They know problems that never show up in online forums.

### How to Get Expert Interviews (Even Without Connections)

| Approach | Script | Success Rate |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| **LinkedIn cold message** | "I'm researching [industry] problems for a project. Could I ask you 3 quick questions? Won't take more than 10 minutes." | ~10-15% |
| **Reddit AMA-style post** | "I'm researching [industry] — what's the #1 thing you wish was easier in your job?" | ~5-20 responses |
| **Friends of friends** | "Do you know anyone who works in [industry]? I'd love to ask them a few questions." | ~30-40% |
| **Twitter/X reply** | Reply to industry professionals' complaints with genuine curiosity | ~5-10% |

### The 5 Questions That Reveal Real Problems

Ask these in this order (they build on each other):

1. **"Walk me through a typical workday."** (Let them talk. Problems emerge naturally.)
2. **"What's the most tedious or frustrating part of your job?"** (Direct pain identification.)
3. **"What tools or processes do you wish worked better?"** (Existing solution gaps.)
4. **"If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about your workflow, what would it be?"** (Aspirational problem — what they dream about solving.)
5. **"Have you ever paid for a tool or service to fix any of these problems?"** (Willingness to pay — the critical question.)

### AI Prompt: Interview Preparation

```text
I'm going to interview a [job title, e.g., "freelance graphic designer"] about
their daily pain points. I want to discover problems I could build a business around.

Help me prepare:
1. Write a brief, friendly intro message I can send to request the interview
2. Create 10 open-ended questions that uncover business-worthy problems
3. List 5 "follow-up prompts" I can use when they mention something interesting
4. Suggest 3 things I should listen for that signal a high-value problem
5. Create a simple template for capturing notes during the interview
```

## Method 6: The Search Data Method

What people search for on Google reveals exactly what they're struggling with. The search bar is the world's largest complaint box.

### Step-by-Step: Google Search Analysis

1. **Start with a seed phrase:** "How to [your niche activity]"
2. **Check Google Autocomplete:** Type your phrase and see what Google suggests
3. **Check "People Also Ask":** The questions in the dropdown are real problems
4. **Check "Related Searches":** At the bottom of search results
5. **Use AnswerThePublic:** Enter your topic and see hundreds of questions visualized

### What to Look For

| Search Pattern | What It Signals | Business Opportunity |
|---------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| "How to..." | People don't know how to do something | Course, guide, template |
| "Best [tool] for..." | People are comparing solutions | Review site, better tool |
| "[Tool] alternative" | People are unhappy with existing tools | A simpler/cheaper/specialized alternative |
| "[Problem] template" | People want a ready-made solution | Template pack, framework |
| "[Problem] freelancer/agency" | People want someone to do it for them | Productized service |

### AI Prompt: Search Data Analysis

```text
I searched Google for terms related to [your niche] and found these
"People Also Ask" questions:

1. [paste question 1]
2. [paste question 2]
3. [paste question 3]
4. [paste question 4]
5. [paste question 5]
6. [paste question 6]
7. [paste question 7]
8. [paste question 8]

Analyze these questions and:
1. Group them by theme (what underlying problems do they reveal?)
2. Which themes appear most frequently?
3. For the top 3 themes, what business could solve these problems?
4. Which of these businesses would be easiest for a solo founder to start?
5. Write a problem statement for the #1 opportunity
```

## 🧪 Exercise: The Pain Point Sprint

You'll now use all 6 methods to build a comprehensive list of pain points. This is the foundation for everything that follows in Phase 1.

### Time Required: 2-3 hours

| Step | Method | Time | Output |
|------|--------|------|--------|
| 1 | Community Mining | 30 min | 5 pain points from Reddit/forums |
| 2 | Review Mining | 30 min | 5 pain points from product reviews |
| 3 | Look in the Mirror | 15 min | 3 pain points from personal experience |
| 4 | Trend Surfing | 20 min | 3 trend-based opportunities |
| 5 | Search Data | 15 min | 5 questions/pain points from Google |
| 6 | AI Synthesis | 20 min | Final ranked list |

### Step 6: AI Synthesis Prompt

After completing steps 1-5, paste all your pain points into this prompt:

```text
I've spent 2 hours researching pain points in the [your niche] space. Here
are all the problems I've found across different research methods:

FROM COMMUNITIES:
[paste your 5]

FROM PRODUCT REVIEWS:
[paste your 5]

FROM PERSONAL EXPERIENCE:
[paste your 3]

FROM TRENDS:
[paste your 3]

FROM SEARCH DATA:
[paste your 5]

Please:
1. Remove any duplicates or overlapping problems
2. Group similar problems into themes
3. Rank the remaining problems by: Pain Severity × Frequency × Market Size
4. Give me a final "Top 10 Pain Points" list
5. For the top 3, write a complete problem statement using this format:
   [WHO] spends/loses [WHAT] every [WHEN] on [PROBLEM] because [CAUSE],
   and their current workaround is [SOLUTION], which is [WHY IT'S BAD].
```

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | What to Do Instead |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Only using one method | Each method reveals different types of problems | Use at least 3 of the 6 methods |
| Surface-level research | Reading 3 Reddit posts isn't research | Read 30+ data points per method |
| Only looking at communities you're already in | Your existing communities may not be where the money is | Deliberately explore communities outside your comfort zone |
| Ignoring problems that seem "too small" | Small, specific problems are often the best businesses | A $50/month tool for a specific niche can be a $1M business |
| Not recording everything | You'll forget the best insights | Use a spreadsheet or structured notes for every finding |
| Skipping the AI synthesis step | Raw data needs analysis to be useful | Let AI help you spot patterns you missed |
