# 1.7 Scoring Opportunities

> "Ideas are cheap. Execution is everything. But smart execution starts with choosing the RIGHT idea." — Chris Sacca

You may have 2-5 promising ideas at this point. You need to kill all but one.

Emotional attachment to an idea is the enemy of good decision-making. This lesson gives you an **objective, numbers-based scoring system** to rank your opportunities like an investor would — removing ego and guesswork from the equation.

---

## Why You Need a Scoring System

Without a scoring system:
- You pick the "exciting" idea (which is often the hardest to execute)
- You flip-flop between ideas for weeks
- You start building, get stuck, and switch to another idea
- You waste months chasing two rabbits and catching neither

With a scoring system:
- You make a data-driven decision in 30 minutes
- You commit with confidence because you can explain WHY
- You have a framework you can re-use whenever you need to evaluate a new opportunity

---

## The LaunchOS Opportunity Score (LOS)

We use a custom scoring framework with 8 criteria across 3 categories. Each criterion is scored 1-10.

### Category 1: Market Strength (Is the opportunity real?)

| Criterion | Score 1-3 | Score 4-6 | Score 7-10 |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|------------|
| **Problem Severity** | "Nice to have" — people shrug | Real frustration, but manageable | Hair-on-fire — costs significant time/money |
| **Market Size** | Very small niche (< 1,000 potential customers) | Moderate market (1,000-50,000) | Large, growing market (50,000+) |
| **Willingness to Pay** | No evidence anyone would pay | People buy adjacent products | People already spend $$ on worse solutions |

### Category 2: Competitive Position (Can you win?)

| Criterion | Score 1-3 | Score 4-6 | Score 7-10 |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|------------|
| **Competitive Gap** | Crowded market, no clear differentiation | Some gaps, but competitors could close them | Clear, defensible gap that competitors are ignoring |
| **Your Advantage** | No relevant skills, network, or experience | Some applicable skills or knowledge | Deep domain expertise, existing audience, or unique access |

### Category 3: Execution Feasibility (Can you actually do this?)

| Criterion | Score 1-3 | Score 4-6 | Score 7-10 |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|------------|
| **Time to First Revenue** | 6+ months before any income | 2-6 months | Could earn money within 30 days |
| **Build Complexity** | Requires coding, team, or significant capital | Needs some learning but doable solo | Can build MVP this weekend with existing tools |
| **Scalability** | Every sale requires your personal time (1:1 trading) | Some manual effort, but can semi-automate | Fully digital — sell one, sell 10,000 with same effort |

### Why the Weights?

| Criterion | Weight | Reasoning |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| Problem Severity | x1.5 | The single most important factor — if the pain isn't real, nothing else matters |
| Willingness to Pay | x1.5 | Critical — separates real businesses from hobby projects |
| Time to First Revenue | x1.5 | For solo founders, fast cash flow is survival |
| Build Complexity | x1.0 | Important but can be managed with the right tools |
| Market Size | x1.0 | Matters, but a small market with high pain beats a big market with low pain |
| Competitive Gap | x1.0 | Important for differentiation |
| Your Advantage | x1.0 | Your personal edge |
| Scalability | x0.5 | Important long-term but less critical for the first 6 months |

### Step 2: Score Each Idea

For each idea, go criterion by criterion and assign honest scores. Use your research from Lessons 1.3-1.6 as evidence.

**Pro tip:** Score each criterion BEFORE calculating the total. This prevents you from unconsciously inflating scores to make your favorite idea win.

### Step 3: Apply the Kill Questions

Even if an idea scores high overall, automatic disqualification applies if:

| Kill Question | If "No"... |
|--------------|-----------|
| **Any criterion scored below 3?** | That criterion is a fatal flaw — the idea needs fundamental rework |
| **Can you build an MVP in 2 weeks?** | Too complex for a first business — simplify or choose something faster |
| **Would YOU pay for this?** | If you wouldn't buy it, why would anyone else? |

## AI-Assisted Scoring

Get an objective second opinion from AI. It won't have all your context, but it can challenge your assumptions.

### AI Prompt: Idea Scoring

```text
I'm using the LaunchOS Opportunity Score to evaluate my business ideas. I need
your objective assessment. Score each idea on a scale of 1-10 for these 8 criteria.

SCORING GUIDE:
- Problem Severity (1=nice-to-have, 10=hair-on-fire)
- Market Size (1=<1000 people, 10=50,000+ people)
- Willingness to Pay (1=no evidence, 10=people already spend $$ on worse solutions)
- Competitive Gap (1=crowded market, 10=clear unfilled niche)
- My Advantage (1=no relevant experience, 10=deep domain expertise)
- Time to First Revenue (1=6+ months, 10=within 30 days)
- Build Complexity (1=requires a team and coding, 10=can build this weekend)
- Scalability (1=pure 1:1 time trading, 10=fully digital, infinite scale)

MY CONTEXT:
Skills: [list your skills]
Time available: [hours per week]
Budget: [$X/month]
Business path: [digital product / service / media / SaaS]

IDEA A: [name and description]
IDEA B: [name and description]
IDEA C: [name and description]

For each idea:
1. Score all 8 criteria with brief justification
2. Calculate the weighted total (Problem Severity x1.5, Willingness to Pay x1.5,
   Time to Revenue x1.5, Scalability x0.5, everything else x1.0)
3. Flag any scores below 3 (kill zone)
4. Declare a winner and explain why

Be brutally honest. I want truth, not encouragement. If all ideas are bad,
tell me they're bad and suggest how to improve the best one.
```

## Worked Example: Scoring 3 Ideas

Let's walk through a real example so you see how this works.

### The Founder's Context
- **Skills:** Marketing, copywriting, social media management
- **Time:** 10 hours/week
- **Budget:** $30/month for tools
- **Path:** Productized service → digital product

### Idea A: Social media management for local dentists ($500/month)

| Criterion | Score | Reasoning |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Problem Severity | 7 | Dentists know they need social media but hate doing it |
| Market Size | 6 | ~200,000 dentist practices in the US, growing |
| Willingness to Pay | 8 | Dentists are used to paying for services, $500/mo is reasonable |
| Competitive Gap | 5 | Some agencies do this, but few specialize in dentists |
| Your Advantage | 7 | Strong marketing skills, can demonstrate value quickly |
| Time to First Revenue | 9 | Could land first client this week with cold outreach |
| Build Complexity | 8 | No building needed — just start doing the work |
| Scalability | 3 | Pure time-trading — hard to scale past 8-10 clients solo |
| **Weighted Total** | **58.5/90** | |

### Idea B: Notion template pack for freelance writers ($49 one-time)

| Criterion | Score | Reasoning |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Problem Severity | 5 | Writers need organization, but it's not urgent pain |
| Market Size | 7 | Millions of freelance writers globally |
| Willingness to Pay | 4 | Writers are price-sensitive; $49 is a stretch for templates |
| Competitive Gap | 4 | Lots of Notion templates already exist on Gumroad |
| Your Advantage | 6 | Good copywriter, knows the audience |
| Time to First Revenue | 7 | Could build and launch in 2-3 weeks |
| Build Complexity | 9 | Just creating Notion templates — very doable |
| Scalability | 9 | Build once, sell infinite times |
| **Weighted Total** | **51.5/90** | |

### Idea C: AI-powered email newsletter for real estate agents ($29/month)

| Criterion | Score | Reasoning |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Problem Severity | 6 | Agents need content but struggle to write consistently |
| Market Size | 7 | ~1.5M real estate agents in the US |
| Willingness to Pay | 7 | Agents spend on marketing tools regularly |
| Competitive Gap | 7 | Few AI-powered newsletter tools for this specific niche |
| Your Advantage | 5 | Marketing skills apply, but no real estate experience |
| Time to First Revenue | 6 | Need to build the system, could take 4-6 weeks |
| Build Complexity | 5 | Requires automation setup, AI prompting, email platform |
| Scalability | 8 | Mostly automated once built |
| **Weighted Total** | **55/90** | |

### The Verdict

**Idea A wins (58.5)** — despite low scalability, it scores highest because it can generate revenue fastest and has proven willingness to pay. The founder could start this TODAY.

**However:** The scalability score of 3 is a flag. The strategy would be: Start with the service (Idea A), learn the market, then build a product (closer to Idea C) based on what you learn from actual clients.

## ⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Hurts | What to Do Instead |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Inflating scores for your favorite idea | You're deceiving yourself with your own data | Score blindly — cover the idea names and score criteria one at a time |
| Only using gut feeling | Gut feeling is useful but biased | Combine gut + data scoring; if they disagree, investigate why |
| Ignoring the kill questions | A fatal flaw in one area sinks the whole business | Take kill questions seriously — a 2 in any area is disqualifying |
| Refusing to drop ideas | "But I love this idea!" — love doesn't pay rent | Drop low scorers fast; you can always come back to them later |
| Over-weighting scalability | Scalability matters later, revenue matters NOW | Focus on ideas that can generate money within 30 days |
| Not using evidence | Scoring from imagination = fantasy | Reference your research from Lessons 1.3-1.6 for every score |
