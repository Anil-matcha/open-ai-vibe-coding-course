# LaunchOS Open-Source Launch Strategy

To get LaunchOS to 10k+ GitHub stars and build a massive community, we cannot just publish the repo and hope people find it. We need a coordinated, multi-channel launch strategy.

This is the playbook to take LaunchOS from a private repo to a trending open-source project.

## Phase 1: Pre-Launch (Building the Hype)
*Goal: Get the first 500 stars from early supporters.*

### 1. The "Build in Public" Twitter/X Thread
- Document the creation of LaunchOS before it launches.
- **Hook:** *"MBA programs cost $150k and teach outdated theory. I spent 100 hours building an open-source, AI-first business curriculum that teaches you how to build a company for $0. It's called LaunchOS, and I'm open-sourcing it next week."*
- Share screenshots of the UI, the templates, and the roadmap.
- Call to Action (CTA): "Reply 'LAUNCH' and I'll DM you the repo link 24 hours before it goes public."

### 2. The Early Contributor Program
- Reach out to 10-15 creators, indie hackers, and developers.
- Ask them to review the repo, fix a typo, or add one tool to `tools.md`.
- By getting them to contribute, they become emotionally invested and will likely share it when it launches.

## Phase 2: Launch Day (The Big Bang)
*Goal: Hit GitHub Trending and reach 2,000+ stars.*

### 1. Hacker News (Show HN)
- **Title:** `Show HN: LaunchOS – An open-source curriculum for building businesses with AI`
- **The Comment:** Write a humble, detailed first comment explaining *why* you built this (frustration with expensive courses, the power of no-code + AI, the desire to make entrepreneurship accessible). Ask for brutal feedback.

### 2. Product Hunt
- Position it as an "Educational Tool" / "AI Resource".
- Create a high-quality 60-second Loom video walking through the repo (show the templates, the curriculum structure).
- Have a recognizable Maker "hunt" the product.

### 3. Reddit (Strategic Cross-Posting)
Post tailored messages to different subreddits. Do NOT spam. Be native.
- `r/Entrepreneur`: "I got tired of $997 business courses, so I open-sourced a curriculum based on AI and no-code."
- `r/SaaS`: "An open-source playbook for building SaaS MVPs without a technical co-founder."
- `r/SideHustle`: "A step-by-step guide to starting a side hustle using AI (Free & Open Source)."

### 4. GitHub Action
- Ensure tags/topics are heavily optimized: `entrepreneurship`, `startup`, `ai`, `no-code`, `curriculum`, `business`.

## Phase 3: Post-Launch (Sustained Growth)
*Goal: Maintain momentum to 10k stars.*

### 1. The Template Virality Loop
- When people use the templates (e.g., The Business Idea Canvas), encourage them to share their completed canvas on Twitter/LinkedIn, tagging `#LaunchOS`. 
- Every shared canvas acts as an advertisement for the repo.

### 2. The Contributor Flywheel
- Add a "Wall of Fame" or use `contrib.rocks` in the README to show the avatars of everyone who contributes.
- Create "Good First Issues" (e.g., "Translate Phase 1 to Spanish", "Add 3 new tools to the directory"). People love contributing to popular repos to build their GitHub profiles.

### 3. Weekly Content Drops
- Instead of releasing all phases at once, release them systematically.
- "Phase 2: Validation is now LIVE in LaunchOS." This gives you an excuse to post about the repo every week for 6 weeks.

### 4. Newsletters
- Pitch LaunchOS to big newsletters in the tech/AI space (e.g., The Rundown, TLDR Newsletter, Ben Bites). 
- Pitch: "The most starred open-source business curriculum on GitHub."
