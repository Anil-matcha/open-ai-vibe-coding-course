# Contributing to LaunchOS

First off, **thank you** for considering contributing to LaunchOS! 

This curriculum relies on the collective intelligence of builders, makers, and entrepreneurs. Whether you're fixing a typo, adding a new template, or writing an entire lesson, your help is what makes this project valuable.

## How You Can Help

Here are the primary ways you can contribute:

### 1. Report Bugs or Typos
Found a broken link? A typo in a lesson? Something that doesn't make sense?
- **Action:** [Open an issue](../../issues) describing what's wrong, or submit a Pull Request fixing it directly.

### 2. Improve Existing Content
Business moves fast. AI moves faster. If a tool we recommend is outdated, or a strategy no longer works:
- **Action:** Submit a PR updating the lesson or `tools.md` file with the latest information.

### 3. Create Templates
Templates are the lifeblood of LaunchOS. They help people take immediate action.
- **Action:** Look at the `templates/` folder. Do you have a framework or worksheet that helped you build your business? Add it!
- **Format:** Ensure it uses clear Markdown and follows the style of existing templates.

### 4. Write New Lessons
If you want to tackle an upcoming lesson on the [Roadmap](roadmap.md):
1. [Open an issue](../../issues) first to claim the lesson and propose your outline.
2. We'll discuss and approve the outline.
3. Submit a PR with the drafted lesson.

### 5. Translate
Help make business education accessible globally.
- **Action:** Propose a new language translation by opening an issue.

## Contribution Guidelines

When writing content for LaunchOS, please follow these principles:

### 1. Be Action-Oriented
LaunchOS is about *doing*, not just reading.
- **Do:** Give concrete steps ("Use this prompt in Claude to generate 10 ideas...")
- **Don't:** Speak only in theory ("It is important to have good ideas...")

### 2. Keep It Simple
Assume the reader has zero technical background and zero business background.
- **Do:** Explain terms simply ("Your MVP is the smallest, fastest version of your product...")
- **Don't:** Use jargon without explaining it ("Optimize your CAC/LTV ratio for series A funding...")

### 3. Emphasize Speed and Validation
We believe in launching fast and learning from real customers.
- **Do:** Encourage putting things in front of people quickly.
- **Don't:** Suggest spending months building before talking to anyone.

### 4. Use AI Practically
Show, don't just tell.
- **Do:** Provide specific, copy-pasteable prompts.
- **Don't:** Just say "Ask AI for help."

### 5. Markdown Style
- Use `>` for important quotes or callouts.
- Use `---` to separate major sections.
- Use bolding for emphasis, but don't overdo it.
- Ensure all links work and open in the current tab (standard Markdown links).

## Step-by-Step Instructions

If you're new to GitHub, here's how to contribute:

1. **Fork the repository** (Click the "Fork" button in the top right).
2. **Clone your fork** locally: `git clone https://github.com/your-username/LaunchOS.git`
3. **Create a branch** for your changes: `git checkout -b feature/your-feature-name`
4. **Make your changes** in your favorite text editor.
5. **Commit your changes**: `git commit -m "Add description of your change"`
6. **Push to your fork**: `git push origin feature/your-feature-name`
7. **Submit a Pull Request** from your fork to our `main` branch.

In your PR description, explain clearly what you changed and why.

## Code of Conduct

By participating in this project, you agree to abide by our Code of Conduct. Be respectful, be helpful, and assume good intent. Harassment or toxic behavior will not be tolerated.
